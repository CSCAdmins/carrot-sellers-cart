## Background

**Born**: April 9, 1945, Glens Falls, New York

**Education**:
- **Harvard College (1967)** — BA in American Government Studies, with Honors
- **Harvard Law School (1970)** — JD; co-founded Harvard Civil Rights Law Review
- **Harvard Divinity School** — Graduate studies in Comparative Social Ethics

**Career**: Constitutional and public interest lawyer for 50+ years. Wall Street (Cahill, Gordon); Chief Trial Counsel, ACLU Rocky Mountain; Legal Counsel, Jesuit U.S. national headquarters (10 years). Currently: President & General Counsel, New Paradigm Institute; Chief Counsel, Romero Institute; Lead Attorney, Lakota People's Law Project.

**Family**: Married to Sara Nelson (Romero Institute co-founder, NPI Executive Director).

### La Penca Case (1986-1988) — Major Setback

Filed $24 million RICO lawsuit naming 29 defendants including Iran-Contra figures (Secord, Hakim, Shackley, Singlaub) and Medellín Cartel leaders (Escobar, Ochoa).

**Outcome**:
- June 23, 1988: Dismissed by Federal Judge James Lawrence King
- Court: "Made no showing of existence of genuine issues of material fact"
- **$955,000 attorney's fees + $79,500 court costs** (Rule 11 sanctions)
- IRS stripped Christic Institute's 501(c)(3) status
- Co-plaintiff Tony Avirgan accused Sheehan of chasing "unsubstantiated wild allegations and conspiracy theories"
